Guardrails: Rule 9.410 (July 1, 2026 text verified) lets the 2DCA strike filings, dismiss the appeal, and award fees for frivolous/bad-faith papers, and § 57.105 (2025) makes a pro se party personally liable for the other side's fees, subject to a 21-day safe harbor — the high-risk category is exactly the sovereign-citizen-style unsworn 'notice and demand'/ownership-declaration genre, while the planned 1.540(b)(4) void-judgment motion is a legitimate low-risk vehicle and agency complaints (DBPR/FLHSMV/AG/JQC/Bar) carry no § 57.105 exposure (Bar complaints are absolutely privileged under Tobkin v. On transcripts: Rule 9.200(b)(1) designations run to the 'approved court reporter, civil court reporter, or approved transcriptionist' who reported the hearing — here no one did, because 6th Circuit county civil hearings are not reported at public expense (Rule 2.535(b); AO 2007-079, under which approval runs through the chief judge/AOC and party recordings are never the official record) — so the controlling vehicle is the Rule 9.200(b)(5) statement of evidence from best available means, served on the opponent (15-day objection window) and settled by the county court before the record transmits (~Aug 2026; initial brief ~Sep 2, 2026), failing which Applegate v. Barnett Bank compels affirmance on any fact-dependent issue.

## GUARDRAIL — Fla. R. App. P. 9.410: sanctions in the 2DCA
- AUTHORITY: Fla. R. App. P. 9.410 (July 1, 2026 compilation) [verified verbatim]
- SAYS: After 10 days' notice the court on its own motion may sanction frivolous/bad-faith filings (reprimand, contempt, striking, dismissal, costs, fees). Party-initiated fee motions ((b)) have a 21-day safe harbor: served first on the target only; filable only if the challenged paper is not withdrawn/corrected within 21 days.
- APPLIES: Every 2DCA filing must be tethered to record facts and real authority — frivolous or repetitive filings risk dismissal of the whole appeal. If served with a sanctions motion, the 21-day safe harbor is the escape hatch — never ignore it. Ask before filing anything: does this request relief the appellate court can actually grant on this record?
- CONFIDENCE: high

## GUARDRAIL — § 57.105 fee-shifting applies to pro se parties; risk ranking of planned filings
- AUTHORITY: Fla. Stat. § 57.105 (2025); Tobkin v. Jarboe, 710 So. 2d 975 (Fla. 1998) [CONFIRMED — § 57.105(1)-(4) verified verbatim on Online Sunshine incl. the 21-day safe harbor; note § 57.105(1)'s attorney-fee split protects only represented parties' LAWYERS — a pro se party bears any award personally; Tobkin holds Bar-complaint filers get absolute immunity so long as no public announcement outside the grievance process]
- SAYS: Courts award the prevailing party fees where a claim or defense was unsupported by material facts or by then-existing law applied to those facts; 21-day safe harbor for party motions.
- APPLIES: Risk ranking: LOW — the 1.540(b)(4) motion (recognized vehicle; fact-based; docket-proved), the filed 2.330 motion (specific sworn statement), the 9.200(b)(5) statements, agency complaints (no § 57.105 exposure; Bar complaints absolutely privileged per Tobkin if kept inside the process). HIGH — unsworn 'notice and demand'/'affidavit of truth'/ownership-declaration filings in either court: nullities at best, sanctions bait at worst, and they burn credibility needed for the real issues.
- CONFIDENCE: high

## GUARDRAIL — what pro se filings get stricken; the Applegate no-transcript trap
- AUTHORITY: Applegate v. Barnett Bank of Tallahassee, 377 So. 2d 1150 (Fla. 1979); Fla. R. App. P. 9.410; uniform treatment of sovereign-citizen theories as frivolous [Applegate CONFIRMED; characterization fair per uniform case law]

## Transcript/transcriptionist rules — what 'approved transcriptionist' means here
- AUTHORITY: Fla. R. App. P. 9.200(b)(1), (b)(5) (July 1, 2026); Fla. R. Gen. Prac. & Jud. Admin. 2.535(a)(1)-(3), (b), (h)(3) (Jan. 1, 2026); 6th Judicial Circuit Admin. Order 2007-079 PA/PI-CIR (Court Reporting) [CONFIRMED — rule text verbatim; AO 2007-079 confirms civil parties 'may use any certified stenographic court reporter,' AOC approval authority, and that CDs/DVDs/party recordings are never the official record]
- SAYS: A 9.200(b) designation presupposes someone actually reported the hearing. In the 6th Circuit, county civil (eviction) hearings are not reported at public expense; a party wanting a record must provide a certified stenographic court reporter; party-made recordings are not the official record.
- APPLIES: The written confirmations from EPReporter@jud6.org (July 7–8) prove no report was made — the trigger for Rule 9.200(b)(5). The July 3 designation (timely) plus those confirmations document diligence. The controlling vehicle is the (b)(5) statement of evidence: serve on Wynne, 15-day objection window, settle in the county court, into the record before transmission.
- CONFIDENCE: high
