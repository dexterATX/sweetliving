# Consumer-protection and regulatory complaint avenues for a defrauded Florida mobile-home purchaser (FDUTPA/Attorney General, FDACS, DBPR mobile-home division, Florida Bar ACAP, JQC)
SUMMARY: All five complaint avenues are free, and none can change the eviction judgment, the writ, or the appeal — but two have real teeth for her fraud narrative. (1) FDUTPA (verified against 2025 statutes): § 501.204(1) outlaws deceptive/unfair practices in trade or commerce; § 501.211 gives a private right to declaratory/injunctive relief ('anyone aggrieved') and actual damages (~$18,000) for a 'person who has suffered a loss'; § 501.2105(1) is two-way prevailing-party fee shifting (a real risk if she loses, an asset for recruiting counsel); limitations is 4 years under § 95.11(3)(e) (2025 renumbering — formerly (3)(f)) from the deceptive act, with no delayed-discovery safety net. Ch. 723 or title-statute violations can be pleaded as per se FDUTPA violations via § 501.203(3)(c). (2) The Attorney General takes complaints online or at PL-01 The Capitol, Tallahassee 32399-1050 (850-414-3990 / 1-866-966-7226) — pattern-enforcement intake, public record; the AG 'does not represent private citizens seeking the return of their money.' (3) FDACS (1-800-435-7352) is the statutory clearinghouse (§ 570.544) — mediates and refers; occasionally shakes loose voluntary refunds. (4) DBPR's mobile-home division is the most on-point regulator for PARK conduct: its form expressly covers 'restrictions on the sale of mobile homes'; § 723.059(1) purchaser approval may not be unreasonably withheld; § 723.006(5) allows cease-and-desist orders, up to $5,000/violation, and class restitution suits — but only a court can judge an eviction; DBPR cannot touch her judgment. (5) Bar/ACAP (866-352-0707; sworn form, 25-page cap, ACAPIntake@floridabar.org) can discipline Wynne only for KNOWING falsity (Rule 4-3.3(a)) or objectively frivolous claims (Rule 4-3.1), will likely defer until the appeal ends, and cannot recover money; the JQC (mail-only, PO Box 14106, Tallahassee 32317) investigates canon violations, is constitutionally confidential, and cannot recuse Judge Compton — recusal is exclusively the pending Rule 2.330 motion. Overriding caution: AG, FDACS, and DBPR complaints are public records opposing counsel can pull — every complaint must match the sworn court filings word-for-word, and court deadlines always come first.

## FDUTPA — unlawful acts and private-claim elements
- AUTHORITY: Fla. Stat. § 501.204(1) (2025); § 501.203(8); elements per the Rollins, Inc. v. Butland line [CONFIRMED]
- SAYS: "Unfair methods of competition, unconscionable acts or practices, and unfair or deceptive acts or practices in the conduct of any trade or commerce are hereby declared unlawful." Private damages claim: (1) deceptive act/unfair practice, (2) causation, (3) actual damages. 'Trade or commerce' includes distributing 'any property, whether tangible or intangible.'
- APPLIES: Taking ~$18,000 for a mobile home while never delivering title, never delivering possession, never obtaining park approval — then evicting the purchaser — is a classic deceptive/unfair pattern; the $18,000 is her actual damages (no punitive or consequential damages under FDUTPA).
- ACTIONS: Frame any FDUTPA count on: payment proof, non-delivery of title (FLHSMV records), park non-approval, and misrepresentations (texts/emails); plead the three elements expressly.
- CONFIDENCE: high

## FDUTPA — standing and remedies (§ 501.211)
- AUTHORITY: Fla. Stat. § 501.211 (2025) [verified verbatim per verifier]
- SAYS: (1) 'anyone aggrieved' may seek declaratory judgment and injunction; (2) a 'person who has suffered a loss' may recover 'actual damages, plus attorney's fees and court costs as provided in s. 501.2105'; (3) the defendant may seek a bond if it alleges the action is frivolous.
- APPLIES: A NEW civil action against Sweet Living, Inc. (and potentially Desmond Surujbali individually if he personally participated — individual liability requires direct participation). Separate from the appeal; extends no appellate deadline.
- CONFIDENCE: high

## FDUTPA — fees (§ 501.2105): two-way risk
- AUTHORITY: Fla. Stat. § 501.2105(1) (2025) [CONFIRMED]
- SAYS: Prevailing party 'after judgment in the trial court and exhaustion of all appeals' may receive fees from the nonprevailing party.
- APPLIES: If she sues and loses, Wynne could seek fees against her; if she wins, the fee statute makes the case attractive to a consumer lawyer (a pro se party cannot recover fees for her own time). Weigh before filing pro se; a well-documented claim minimizes the risk.
- CONFIDENCE: high

## FDUTPA — limitations
- AUTHORITY: Fla. Stat. § 95.11(3)(e) (2025) ('action founded on a statutory liability' — four years; renumbered from (3)(f) by the 2023 amendments) [verified]
- SAYS/APPLIES: Four years from the deceptive act/payment (Dec 2025 payments → deadline ~Dec 2029); delayed discovery generally unavailable for FDUTPA. No urgency, but calendar it from the earliest payment/misrepresentation.
- CONFIDENCE: high

## FDUTPA — per se violations via predicate statutes
- AUTHORITY: Fla. Stat. § 501.203(3)(c) (2025) [CONFIRMED]
- SAYS: A FDUTPA 'violation' includes violation of 'any law, statute, rule, regulation, or ordinance which proscribes unfair methods of competition, or unfair, deceptive, or unconscionable acts or practices.'
- APPLIES: Plead ch. 723 (§§ 723.058, 723.059(1)) and ch. 319 title-transfer violations as per se FDUTPA counts alongside the traditional deceptive-act count (predicate must proscribe deceptive/unfair conduct — case-specific).
- CONFIDENCE: medium

## Florida Attorney General complaint — how and what to expect
- AUTHORITY: AG consumer complaint process (myfloridalegal.com); §§ 501.203(2), 501.206-.208, 501.2075, 501.2077 [form language verified]
- SAYS: File online or mail to Office of the Attorney General, PL-01 The Capitol, Tallahassee, FL 32399-1050; (850) 414-3990; 1-866-9NO-SCAM. One business per form; copies not originals. The form states the AG 'does not represent private citizens seeking the return of their money'; submissions are public record (ch. 119); false statements are a misdemeanor (§ 837.06); penalties can be enhanced for victimizing seniors/disabled/military.
- APPLIES: File against Sweet Living, Inc.; cross-reference the FLHSMV report and DBPR complaint. Value: official record + pattern enforcement (up to $10,000/violation under § 501.2075) — especially potent given Sweet Living's 19 similar eviction cases. Keep it factual and consistent with sworn filings.
- URL: https://www.myfloridalegal.com/consumer-protection/consumer-complaint-form
- CONFIDENCE: high

## FDACS — statewide clearinghouse (1-800-HELP-FLA)
- AUTHORITY: Fla. Stat. § 570.544 (2025); fdacs.gov complaint portal [CONFIRMED]
- SAYS: FDACS 'shall serve as a clearinghouse' for consumer protection; transmits complaints to the agency most directly concerned; mediates where no other agency has jurisdiction; refers prima facie criminal evidence to Legal Affairs/law enforcement.
- APPLIES: Duplicative of AG/DBPR but useful: mediation sometimes produces voluntary refunds; routing help; the criminal-referral pathway matters given the title-fraud report. ⚠️ If any mediated refund offer arrives, do not sign a release that could waive the appeal or FDUTPA claims without careful reading.
- URL: https://www.fdacs.gov/Contact-Us/File-a-Complaint
- CONFIDENCE: high

## DBPR mobile-home division — complaint against the park (ch. 723)
- AUTHORITY: DBPR Mobile Home Complaint Form; ch. 723; Fla. Admin. Code chs. 61B-29–35 [CORRECTED — mail to 2601 Blair Stone Road, Tallahassee, FL 32399-1030, (850) 487-9948 per the form itself; (850) 488-1122 is the Division's general line]
- SAYS: The Division investigates only ch. 723 / 61B-29–35 violations; the routinely-investigated list includes 'Restrictions on the sale of mobile homes'; complaints are public records ('the Division cannot protect your identity'); jurisdiction covers parks with 10+ lots; status notice within 30 days of receipt (§ 723.006(6)).
- APPLIES: Target the PARK OWNER's conduct: § 723.059(1) (purchaser approval 'may not be unreasonably withheld' — Bergert's Dec 17 denial and the never-processed application), § 723.058 (sale restrictions). Identify whether Sweet Living, Inc. is the park owner of record first. Answer the title questions honestly (no title; explain why; attach FLHSMV correspondence); disclose the court cases.
- URL: https://www2.myfloridalicense.com/lsc/documents/mobilehomecomplaintform.pdf
- CONFIDENCE: high

## DBPR powers and hard limits
- AUTHORITY: Fla. Stat. § 723.006(5)(b), (5)(d), (5)(e)1., (6) (2025); DBPR program statements [CONFIRMED]
- SAYS: Cease-and-desist + affirmative action orders; class actions in circuit court for declaratory/injunctive relief or restitution; civil penalties up to $5,000 per violation. DBPR's own site: it cannot judge rent reasonableness and cannot intervene in a court eviction — 'Only a court of law can determine if the park owner's reason for evicting the resident is valid.'
- APPLIES: DBPR is a pressure/record-building tool — it cannot vacate the judgment, review the writ, or return the $18,000 individually on any fast timeline. Do not delay any court filing waiting for DBPR.
- CONFIDENCE: high

## Florida Bar ACAP — complaint against opposing counsel
- AUTHORITY: Bar Inquiry/Complaint Form + ACAP process; R. Regulating Fla. Bar 3-7.16(a)(1) (6-year limit) [CONFIRMED]
- SAYS: ACAP hotline 1-866-352-0707 (free); mail to The Florida Bar, Attn.: ACAP, 651 E. Jefferson St., Tallahassee, FL 32399-2300, or ACAPIntake@floridabar.org; max 25 pages incl. exhibits; one attorney per form; chronological, fact-specific, signed under penalty of perjury (unsworn complaints not considered). The Bar cannot intervene in the case; complaints arising from pending litigation are routinely DEFERRED until the case and appeal conclude; ~25% of inquiries open a disciplinary file.
- APPLIES: A complaint against Wynne rests on Rules 4-3.3/4-3.1 and needs evidence of KNOWING falsity — e.g., documents in his possession showing no rental agreement, the park's own Dec 31 Notice of Denial of Tenancy, the Feb 5 rent notice to Sally Potes for the same lot, correspondence telling him there was no tenancy. Even success disciplines the lawyer only — it does not vacate anything. Call ACAP first; expect deferral; never let it distract from court deadlines.
- DEADLINE: 6 years from discovery (~2032) — no urgency.
- URL: https://www.floridabar.org/public/acap/filing-a-complaint/
- CONFIDENCE: high

## What attorney conduct is sanctionable — Rules 4-3.3 and 4-3.1
- AUTHORITY: R. Regulating Fla. Bar 4-3.3(a)-(b), 4-3.1 (RRTFB June 30, 2026 edition) [text verified]
- SAYS: 4-3.3(a): a lawyer shall not KNOWINGLY make false statements of fact or law to a tribunal, fail to correct his own material false statements, fail to disclose directly adverse controlling authority, or offer evidence known to be false. 4-3.1: no frivolous claims — there must be a non-frivolous basis in law and fact.
- APPLIES: 'Knowingly' is the operative word. The strongest angles: affirmatively false factual statements in the complaint (rent from Dec 1, 2025 — before she bought the home; the Jan 5 notice's 'currently residing' claim) and anything represented at the untranscribed hearings — which is another reason the Rule 9.200(b)(5) statements matter: they double as the only record of what was said.
- URL: https://www.floridabar.org/rules/rrtfb/
- CONFIDENCE: high

## JQC complaint against the judge — process and limits
- AUTHORITY: Florida JQC complaint process; Fla. Const. art. V, § 12 [process details verified on floridajqc.com]
- SAYS: MAIL ONLY (no phone/fax/email) — official form, one judge per form, original signed + unbound copies, to Florida JQC, P.O. Box 14106, Tallahassee, FL 32317; (850) 488-1581. The JQC investigates judicial MISCONDUCT (canon violations); files are constitutionally confidential unless formal charges are filed; only the Florida Supreme Court imposes discipline.
- APPLIES: The JQC cannot vacate the judgment, reverse the stay denial, or remove Judge Compton from the case — recusal is exclusively the Rule 2.330 motion/prohibition track. A viable complaint describes specific conduct (the 'Desmond needs his money' statement; default entered over filed answers; refusal to review the ordered title evidence), not disagreement with rulings. Filing a JQC complaint is NOT itself a ground for disqualification. Lowest priority; no effect on the case.
- URL: https://floridajqc.com/complaint-process/
- CONFIDENCE: high

## Cross-cutting cautions
- AUTHORITY: Fla. Stat. ch. 119 (public records); § 837.06 (false official statements); R. Regulating Fla. Bar 3-7.16 (deferral); art. V, § 12 (JQC confidentiality) [CONFIRMED]
- APPLIES: Everything filed with the AG, FDACS, and DBPR is a public record Wynne can pull — draft every agency complaint AFTER finalizing the sworn court narrative and keep one master chronology so all filings match word-for-word (same dates, same amounts, same characterizations). Sequencing: (1) court deadlines first; (2) DBPR + AG (highest relevance); (3) FDACS (clearinghouse/mediation); (4) Bar/JQC last (no effect on the case outcome).
