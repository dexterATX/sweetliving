# Stays pending appeal of a Florida residential eviction judgment (Fla. R. App. P. 9.310; Fla. Stat. §§ 83.60, 83.62; mootness; 2DCA motion practice)
SUMMARY: Rodgers has a clear, immediately available remedy: because the Pasco County Court denied her stay on June 30, 2026, Fla. R. App. P. 9.310(f) (verified against the official July 1, 2026 compilation) lets her file a separate-document MOTION in the 2DCA asking it to review that denial; the 2DCA reviews for abuse of discretion (Mariner Health Care v. Baker, 739 So. 2d 608) applying the two stay factors — likelihood of success on appeal and likelihood of harm absent a stay (St. Lucie County, 444 So. 2d 1133; Perez, 769 So. 2d 389). Her success prong is unusually strong: on May 29, 2026, in Zielinski v. Palermo, 2D2025-1009, the 2DCA reversed this same Pasco county court for entering a default eviction judgment without an evidentiary hearing on a pending motion to determine rent under § 83.60(2) — the apparent error here, since her June 1 rent motion preceded the default. No statute requires registry deposits to stay a residential eviction judgment pending appeal (that is a discretionary 9.310(a) condition, commonly imposed and strictly enforced per Stetson, 18 So. 3d 717), and under Alfonso/Zielinski her payment/no-monies-due defense plus rent motion removed any trial-level deposit duty. The harm prong is acute: § 83.62 (2025) allows writ execution on 24 hours' posted notice (weekends don't pause it) with immunized removal of her personal property. Finally, even if the writ executes, binding 2DCA precedent holds the appeal is NOT moot because eviction judgments carry collateral housing/credit consequences (Jappa, 346 So. 3d 222, 224; reaffirmed in Zielinski 2026). Filing mechanics: e-file the emergency motion in case 2D2026-1666 via myflcourtaccess.com (pro se filers may also paper-file), with a Rule 9.300(a)-compliant body, Rule 9.220 appendix, emergency notice to opposing counsel, and an interim-stay request; no new fee is due (the $300 fee under § 35.22(2)(a) attaches to the appeal and awaits her pending § 57.082 indigency determination per Rule 9.430(a)). The motion does not toll the briefing clock — her initial brief remains due about September 2, 2026.

## Rule 9.310(a) — discretionary stay; motion must go to the lower tribunal first; stay may be conditioned
- AUTHORITY: Fla. R. App. P. 9.310(a) (July 1, 2026 compilation) [CONFIRMED]
- SAYS: Verbatim: "(a) Application in Lower Tribunal. Except as provided by general law and in subdivision (b) of this rule, a party seeking to stay a final or nonfinal order pending review first must file a motion in the lower tribunal, which has continuing jurisdiction, in its discretion, to grant, modify, or deny such relief. A stay pending review may be conditioned on the posting of a good and sufficient bond, other conditions, or both."
- APPLIES: Rodgers satisfied the file-below-first prerequisite: the county court denied her stay June 30, 2026. The county court retains "continuing jurisdiction" over stay issues even while the appeal is pending, so a renewed/modified stay motion below (e.g., before a successor judge if disqualification is granted) is also permissible.
- URL: https://www-media.floridabar.org/uploads/2026/06/Appellate-Court-Rules-07-01-26.pdf (pp. 173-176)
- CONFIDENCE: high

## Rule 9.310(b)(1) — the automatic money-judgment bond stay does NOT apply to a possession judgment
- AUTHORITY: Fla. R. App. P. 9.310(b)(1) (July 1, 2026 compilation) [CONFIRMED]
- SAYS: The automatic-stay-by-bond mechanism is limited by its terms to judgments "solely for the payment of money." (Note: SC2025-0241 amends (b)(1) effective Sept. 1, 2026; (a) and (f) unchanged.)
- APPLIES: The eviction judgment awards possession, so there is no as-of-right supersedeas by bond; the only path is the discretionary route.
- CONFIDENCE: high

## Rule 9.310(f) — review of a stay denial by MOTION in the 2DCA
- AUTHORITY: Fla. R. App. P. 9.310(f) (July 1, 2026 compilation) [verified verbatim per verifier]
- SAYS: Verbatim: "(f) Review. A party may seek review of a lower tribunal's order entered under this rule by filing a motion in the court. The motion must be filed as a separate document." 1977 Committee Note: "The court has plenary power to alter any requirements imposed by the lower tribunal," and the rule is "not intended to limit the constitutional power of the court to issue stay orders after its jurisdiction has been invoked."
- APPLIES: The vehicle for reviewing the June 30 denial — stand-alone document, no new case or fee; the 2DCA can grant interim relief. (Superseded in practice by the July 9 writ execution, but preserved for any future writ.)
- CONFIDENCE: high

## Standard on 9.310(f) review: abuse of discretion; two stay factors
- AUTHORITY: Mariner Health Care of Nashville, Inc. v. Baker, 739 So. 2d 608 (Fla. 1st DCA 1999); St. Lucie County v. North Palm Development Corp., 444 So. 2d 1133, 1135 (Fla. 4th DCA 1984); Perez v. Perez, 769 So. 2d 389 (Fla. 3d DCA 1999) [CORRECTED — all three cases verified; sourcing chain corrected: the Bar Journal article cites only Perez, and Perez relies on State ex rel. Price v. McCord, 380 So. 2d 1037 (Fla. 1980); substantive law accurate]
- SAYS: The DCA reviews a stay ruling deferentially for abuse of discretion (Mariner). The stay factors are (a) likelihood of success on the merits of the appeal and (b) likelihood of harm if the stay is not granted (St. Lucie County at 1135; Perez).
- APPLIES: Likelihood of success is unusually strong on this record: default judgment despite the pending June 1 motion to determine rent — the precise Zielinski error, against the same court. Denial without weighing the mandatory factors is itself an abuse of discretion.
- CONFIDENCE: medium

## Fla. Stat. § 83.62 (2025) — writ of possession mechanics
- AUTHORITY: Fla. Stat. § 83.62 (2025) [CONFIRMED]
- SAYS: "(1) ...the clerk shall issue a writ to the sheriff describing the premises and commanding the sheriff to put the landlord in possession after 24 hours' notice conspicuously posted on the premises. Saturdays, Sundays, and legal holidays do not stay the 24-hour notice period. (2) At the time the sheriff executes the writ... the landlord or the landlord's agent may remove any personal property found on the premises to or near the property line... Neither the sheriff nor the landlord... shall be liable... for the loss, destruction, or damage to the property after it has been removed." (History through ch. 2013-136; no 2024–2026 amendment.)
- APPLIES: The July 8 posting → July 9 execution (28.5 hours) was mechanically compliant with the 24-hour floor; the attack on the eviction lies upstream in the judgment. § 83.62(2)'s property-removal immunity is the concrete-harm fact; note the mobile home itself is Rodgers' titled personal property per DIN 44 Finding 1.

- CONFIDENCE: high

## Fla. Stat. § 83.60(2) (2025) + the Zielinski line — no deposit required where payment/no-tenancy is raised and a rent motion is pending; default without an evidentiary hearing is reversible error
- AUTHORITY: Fla. Stat. § 83.60(2) (2025); Zielinski v. Palermo, No. 2D2025-1009 (Fla. 2d DCA May 29, 2026); Alfonso v. Maiuri, 405 So. 3d 527, 529-30 (Fla. 3d DCA 2025); Jappa v. Master Lessee Mezzo, LLC, 346 So. 3d 222, 223-24 (Fla. 2d DCA 2022); Kaufman v. High Seas, LLC, 383 So. 3d 509, 513 (Fla. 4th DCA 2024); Prince v. MCR Apts. 1, LLC, 326 So. 3d 228 (Fla. 3d DCA 2021); Axen v. Poah Cutler Manor, LLC, 323 So. 3d 800, 801 n.1 (Fla. 3d DCA 2021); Ford v. Princeton Groves FL Apartments, No. 3D24-1747 (Fla. 3d DCA May 7, 2025) [CONFIRMED — Zielinski read in full; quotes verbatim]
- SAYS: Zielinski (reversing the Pasco County Court): "where a tenant alleges 'payment'—or that no monies are due—and files a motion to determine rent, there is no statutory requirement that any monies be paid into the court registry... the trial court is required to hold an evidentiary hearing; if the trial court enters a default final judgment without first conducting an evidentiary hearing to resolve a pending motion to determine rent that raises disputed issues of fact, the trial court commits reversible error."
- APPLIES: Rodgers' verified motion to determine rent + existence-of-tenancy hearing request (DIN 26) preceded both judgments; DIN 41 ¶B denied it as "legally deficient" without a hearing, and both judgments rested the "judicial default" on non-deposit. This is the lead merits issue for the appeal and the 1.540(b) motion. § 83.60(2)'s deposit obligation also runs only "during the pendency of the proceeding" in the trial court — it does not govern stays pending appeal.
- URL: http://www.leg.state.fl.us/statutes/index.cfm?App_mode=Display_Statute&URL=0000-0099/0083/Sections/0083.60.html ; Zielinski: https://flcourts-media.flcourts.gov/content/download/2489389/opinion/Opinion_2025-1009.pdf
- CONFIDENCE: high

## Deposit conditions on stays — discretionary, strictly enforced
- AUTHORITY: Fla. R. App. P. 9.310(a); Stetson Management Co. v. Fiddler's Elbow, Inc., 18 So. 3d 717 (Fla. 2d DCA 2009) [existence confirmed; residential-vs-nonresidential context flagged low-confidence]
- SAYS: No ch. 83 Part II provision conditions an appellate stay on registry deposits (contrast § 83.232 for nonresidential); any deposit condition comes from 9.310(a)'s "other conditions" language. Stetson: missing a court-ordered monthly deposit entitled the landlord to immediate writ issuance — blowing a condition forfeits the stay.
- APPLIES: If any conditioned stay is ever granted, comply to the letter and keep clerk receipts.
- CONFIDENCE: medium

## Mootness — execution of the writ does NOT moot the eviction appeal (binding 2DCA authority)
- AUTHORITY: Jappa, 346 So. 3d 222, 224 (Fla. 2d DCA 2022); Zielinski (2d DCA 2026); Ford (3d DCA 2025) n.2; Alfonso (3d DCA 2025); Godwin v. State, 593 So. 2d 211, 212 (Fla. 1992) [CONFIRMED — quotes verified in the opinions]
- SAYS: Jappa: "Although Jappa has already been evicted and has moved, we conclude that this appeal is not moot because the eviction can have collateral consequences for Jappa when she seeks to obtain housing or credit." Zielinski applies the same holding. Ford n.2 likewise; on reversal, Ford ordered the judgment AND writ vacated. Doctrinal root: Godwin (collateral legal consequences defeat mootness).
- APPLIES: The writ executed July 9, 2026 — appeal 2D2026-1666 survives; Sweet Living cannot obtain dismissal on mootness grounds under the 2DCA's own recent precedent. Cite Jappa/Zielinski preemptively in the initial brief and request vacatur of the writ and restoration of possession per Ford on reversal.
- CONFIDENCE: high

## Mechanics for any 2DCA motion practice (pro se, indigency pending)
- AUTHORITY: Fla. R. App. P. 9.310(f), 9.300(a), (c), (d)(2), 9.420; Fla. Stat. § 35.22(2)(a) (2025) [CORRECTED — all verified except eDCA reference: ACIS (acis.flcourts.gov) replaced eDCA for the 2DCA effective April 15, 2024; self-represented litigants register with ACIS]
- SAYS: Motions in the existing case carry no new fee (the one-time $300 fee attaches to the appeal, waivable on indigency). Contents per 9.300(a): grounds, relief sought, argument, citations; appendix with affidavits allowed. Emergency relief: reasonable notice to all parties (9.300(c)). Opposing party has 15 days to respond; the court may shorten. Stay motions do not toll the timetable (9.300(d)(2)). 2DCA clerk: (727) 610-3741, 525 Mirror Lake Drive N., St. Petersburg, FL 33701.
- CONFIDENCE: high

## Indigency on appeal — Rule 9.430(a) and § 57.082
- AUTHORITY: Fla. R. App. P. 9.430(a) (July 1, 2026); Fla. Stat. § 57.082 (2025) [CONFIRMED]
- SAYS: The application goes to the clerk of the LOWER tribunal on the supreme-court-approved form; the clerk's reasons for denial must be in writing and are reviewable by the lower tribunal; review of the lower tribunal's decision is by motion in the appellate court. § 57.082: indigent if household income ≤ 200% of federal poverty guidelines; a petition to review a nonindigency determination carries no filing fee.
- APPLIES: Rodgers' application (signed Jul 7) is in the right place; if denied, the review chain is clerk → county court → motion in the 2DCA. The 2DCA's June 26 fee order (40 days → ~Aug 5) is the governing deadline.
