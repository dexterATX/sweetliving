# Judicial disqualification in Florida trial courts — Fla. R. Gen. Prac. & Jud. Admin. 2.330 (current Jan. 1, 2026 text) and Fla. Stat. § 38.10; prohibition review of a denial
SUMMARY: Current Rule 2.330 (verified from the official July 1, 2026 compilation, text dated Jan. 1, 2026) governs Rodgers's July 7 motion to disqualify county Judge Compton, and its lettering has changed from what older sources say: grounds are now (e), service on the judge is (d), legal-sufficiency-only determination is (h), successive motions (i), successor-judge reconsideration of prior rulings is (j) with a 30-day (not 20-day) window, and the immediate-ruling/deemed-granted provision is (l). The motion must be sworn/affirmed by the party (pro se needs no attorney certificate), state the precise discovery date, and be filed within 20 days of discovering the grounds and promptly served on the judge; the judge must take the sworn facts as true, may rule only on legal sufficiency, and must act immediately and no later than 30 days after SERVICE — if the motion is not DENIED within 30 days of service, it is deemed granted and Rodgers may seek an order directing the clerk to reassign the case. While the motion is pending Judge Compton may take no other action (Berry v. Berry; post-motion orders are vacated per Wishoff, subject to Fischer's ministerial exception), and if his denial order argues with her allegations that itself creates a new ground (Bundy v. Rudd; Dominguez). Review of a denial is by petition for writ of prohibition — a new original proceeding in the DCA under Rules 9.030(b)(3) and 9.100 (de novo review, judge omitted from caption but named in body and served, Rule 9.220 appendix of the sworn motion and denial order — no transcript needed, indigency waiver under 9.430, no fixed deadline but prompt filing required, and a show-cause order automatically stays the county court). Because erroneous denials get only harmless-error review on later appeal (Davis v. State, 2022), prohibition is the remedy to use. Section 38.10 supplies the substantive right ("the judge shall proceed no further") but Rule 2.330 controls procedure (Livingston). Biggest substantive risk: adverse rulings alone are legally insufficient grounds (Downs v. Moore) — the motion properly rests on the specific "Desmond needs his money" bench statement discovered June 18.

## Grounds — subdivision (e) in the CURRENT rule (formerly (d))
- AUTHORITY: Rule 2.330(e) (2026); Downs v. Moore, 801 So. 2d 906 (Fla. 2001); Correll v. State, 698 So. 2d 522, 524-25 (Fla. 1997); R.M.C. v. D.C., 77 So. 3d 234 (Fla. 1st DCA 2012); Kline v. JRD Mgmt. Corp., 165 So. 3d 812 (Fla. 1st DCA 2015) [rule text verified; case line standard]
- SAYS: The motion must set forth "all specific and material facts upon which the judge's impartiality might reasonably be questioned," including a reasonable fear of not receiving a fair trial "because of specifically described prejudice or bias of the judge." Adverse rulings alone are legally insufficient (Downs; Correll). Sufficiency test: facts that would create in a reasonably prudent person a well-founded fear of not receiving a fair and impartial trial.
- APPLIES: The filed motion correctly rests on the specific bench statement ("Desmond needs his money") rather than adverse rulings, and expressly disclaims the rulings as independent grounds — the right structure. A judge's expressed alignment with one party's financial interest before the disputed issues (whether tenancy existed and whether rent was owed) were tried is classic well-founded-fear material.
- CONFIDENCE: high

## 20-day filing window from discovery
- AUTHORITY: Rule 2.330(g) (2026) (10→20 days by SC20-165, eff. Mar. 1, 2021) [CONFIRMED]
- SAYS: "A motion to disqualify shall be filed within a reasonable time not to exceed 20 days after discovery... of the facts constituting the grounds for the motion."
- APPLIES: Ground discovered June 18 (the statement was made in her presence at the hearing); filed July 7 — within 20 days. New grounds (e.g., a denial order that argues with her) trigger a fresh 20-day window.
- CONFIDENCE: high

## Judge decides LEGAL SUFFICIENCY ONLY; arguing with the facts is itself a new ground
- AUTHORITY: Rule 2.330(h) (2026); Bundy v. Rudd, 366 So. 2d 440, 442 (Fla. 1978); Dominguez v. State, 944 So. 2d 1052 (Fla. 4th DCA 2006); Barwick v. State, 660 So. 2d 685, 694 (Fla. 1995) (status-of-record exception) [rule verified verbatim; Bundy quote verified]
- SAYS: The judge "may determine only the legal sufficiency of the motion and shall not pass on the truth of the facts alleged"; a denial order "shall not take issue with the motion." Bundy: a judge who "attempted to refute the charges of partiality... has then exceeded the proper scope of his inquiry and on that basis alone established grounds for his disqualification."
- APPLIES: The sworn facts are taken as true. If any denial order argues with, refutes, or characterizes the "Desmond needs his money" allegation, that alone creates a fresh ground — immediately file a renewed motion citing Bundy/Dominguez and use the order in the prohibition petition.
- CONFIDENCE: high

## 30-day ruling deadline; failure = DEEMED GRANTED (now subdivision (l))
- AUTHORITY: Rule 2.330(l) (2026); Tableau Fine Art Group v. Jacoboni, 853 So. 2d 299 (Fla. 2003); Fuster-Escalona v. Wisotsky, 781 So. 2d 1063 (Fla. 2000) [rule verified verbatim]
- SAYS: "The judge... shall take action on the motion immediately, but no later than 30 days after the service of the motion as set forth in subdivision (d). If the motion is not denied within 30 days of service, the motion is deemed granted and the moving party may seek an order from the court directing the clerk to reassign the case."
- APPLIES: Once service on the judge is confirmed/effected, calendar 30 days. If no denial by then: file a "Notice that Motion to Disqualify Is Deemed Granted Under Rule 2.330(l)" plus a motion for an order directing the Pasco clerk to reassign the case.
- DEADLINE: 30 days from confirmed service on the judge.
- CONFIDENCE: high

## Case activity while the motion is pending — judge must rule on it FIRST
- AUTHORITY: Berry v. Berry, 765 So. 2d 855 (Fla. 5th DCA 2000); Wishoff v. Polen, 468 So. 2d 1035 (Fla. 4th DCA 1985); Fischer v. Knuck, 497 So. 2d 240 (Fla. 1986); Godin v. Owens, 275 So. 3d 700 (Fla. 5th DCA 2019) [CORRECTED — Fischer disapproved Wishoff to the extent of conflict; the ministerial exception stands: a judge may reduce to writing rulings orally announced BEFORE the motion was filed]
- SAYS: Berry: "A court must first rule on the motion before deciding any other motions or proceeding further with the case." Wishoff: a final judgment entered after the disqualification motion was filed must be vacated (sound where the post-motion order involves new exercises of discretion). Fischer/Godin: ministerial memorialization of pre-motion oral rulings is permitted.
- APPLIES: From July 7 forward, Judge Compton may not decide anything else (including the 1.540(b)(4) motion or fee/indigency review) until he rules on disqualification. Any substantive post-July 7 order (other than memorializing a pre-July 7 oral ruling) is subject to vacatur. The June 30 stay-denial order predates the motion; Rule 2.330(j) reconsideration is the tool for that one.
- ACTIONS: Monitor the docket daily; move to vacate any non-ministerial post-motion order citing Berry/Wishoff.
- CONFIDENCE: high

## Review of a DENIAL: petition for writ of prohibition
- AUTHORITY: Bundy, 366 So. 2d at 442; Sutton v. State, 975 So. 2d 1073 (Fla. 2008); Joshua v. State, 254 So. 3d 419 (Fla. 4th DCA 2018); Murphy v. Collins, 307 So. 3d 102 (Fla. 3d DCA 2020); Fla. R. App. P. 9.030(b)(3), 9.100(a),(b),(e),(g),(h), 9.220, 9.430 (July 1, 2026 ed.) [rules verified verbatim]
- SAYS: Prohibition is "both an appropriate and necessary remedy" for a wrongly denied disqualification motion (Bundy); denial of an INITIAL motion is reviewed de novo. Procedure: new original proceeding in the DCA; the judge's name is OMITTED from the caption but the judge is a formal party named in the body and served; Rule 9.220 appendix (the sworn motion, the denial order, docket) — no transcript needed; Rule 9.100(h): a show-cause order automatically stays the lower tribunal; no fixed deadline but file promptly (laches risk); Rule 9.430 indigency waiver available.
- APPLIES: If denied, file the prohibition petition in the 2DCA within days. The missing transcript is no obstacle — the sworn motion's facts are taken as true. Pre-draft the petition and appendix now.
- CONFIDENCE: high

## Why prohibition beats waiting for the merits appeal
- AUTHORITY: Davis v. State, 347 So. 3d 315 (Fla. 2022) [CONFIRMED]
- SAYS: "[T]he erroneous denial of a legally sufficient motion for disqualification... is not reversible error per se" — harmless-error analysis applies when raised on appeal after judgment.
- APPLIES: Don't fold the issue into appeal 2D2026-1666; immediate prohibition review is the stronger route, and a granted writ removes Judge Compton from all future proceedings including the 1.540(b)(4) motion and the 9.200(b)(5) statement settlement.
- CONFIDENCE: high

## After disqualification: successor judge may reconsider prior rulings — 30-day window
- AUTHORITY: Rule 2.330(j) (2026) (20→30 days by SC20-165); Rath v. Network Mktg., L.C., 944 So. 2d 485 (Fla. 4th DCA 2006) [CONFIRMED]
- SAYS: "Prior factual or legal rulings by a disqualified judge may be reconsidered and vacated or amended by a successor judge based upon a motion for reconsideration, which must be filed within 30 days of the order of disqualification, unless good cause is shown..." Rath: reconsideration exists "to remove the taint of prejudice," not for a second bite.
- APPLIES: If granted/deemed granted: within 30 days of the disqualification order, move the successor judge to reconsider the June 30 stay denial and the rent-determination denial. CAVEAT: the final judgment itself is on appeal — the county court's power over IT is constrained by Rule 9.600 absent relinquishment; target the collateral/post-judgment orders. Pre-draft now.
- DEADLINE: 30 days from any future disqualification order.
- CONFIDENCE: high

## Successive motions against a SUCCESSOR judge are harder
- AUTHORITY: Rule 2.330(i) (2026); Fla. Stat. § 38.10 (2025) (second part); Carnevale v. Rogenia Trading, Inc., 365 So. 3d 426 (Fla. 3d DCA 2023); Delgado v. Miller, 358 So. 3d 801 (Fla. 3d DCA 2023) [rule and statute verified]
- SAYS: A successor judge cannot be disqualified on a successive motion by the same party unless the successor judge rules that he or she is in fact not fair or impartial — the successor may pass on the truth of the alleged facts; review is abuse-of-discretion; "[p]rohibition does not lie unless the record clearly refutes the successor judge's decision."
- APPLIES: Essentially one favorable presumption. Do not file a second disqualification motion against a successor judge absent genuinely new, provable bias.
- CONFIDENCE: high

## § 38.10 interplay: statute gives the right; Rule 2.330 controls procedure
- AUTHORITY: Fla. Stat. § 38.10 (2025); Livingston v. State, 441 So. 2d 1083 (Fla. 1983) [CONFIRMED]
- SAYS: § 38.10: on a legally sufficient affidavit of prejudice, "the judge shall proceed no further, but another judge shall be designated." Livingston: the statute confers a substantive right; the process is controlled by the court rule; a § 38.10-styled motion is not invalid. The statute's "certificate of counsel of record" does not block a pro se movant.
- APPLIES: Cite both § 38.10 and Rule 2.330 (the filed motion already does), but comply with the RULE's mechanics.
