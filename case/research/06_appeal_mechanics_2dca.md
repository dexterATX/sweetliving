# Second DCA appeal mechanics for a final county-court civil (eviction) judgment — deadlines, rendition/tolling, show-cause practice, fees/indigency, jurisdiction, motions/extensions, e-filing (Rodgers v. Sweet Living, Inc., 2D2026-1666)
SUMMARY: The appeal is in the right court and timely. Since ch. 2020-61, Laws of Florida (§ 3 amending § 26.012; § 8 repealing § 924.08, both effective Jan. 1, 2021), final county-court civil judgments appeal directly to the Second DCA under Rule 9.030(b)(1)(A) and art. V, § 4(b)(1). Rule 9.110(b)'s 30-day jurisdictional clock runs from rendition (signed written judgment filed with the Pasco clerk — DIN 44 was rendered June 18, 2026, so the June 24 notice was timely). A Rule 1.540 motion does NOT toll rendition (absent from the exclusive 9.020(h)(1) list); orders on 1.540 motions are separately appealable under Rule 9.130(a)(5) with no rehearing tolling. A premature notice is protected by Rule 9.110(l), and non-jurisdictional notice defects are curable by amendment under Rule 9.040(d)/(f) — the July 3 amended notice + July 8 transmittal cured the failure to attach the order appealed (the 2DCA discharged its show-cause order July 7); what an amendment cannot cure is an untimely original notice. The June 26 fee order gives 40 days (→ ~Aug 5) to pay $300 (§ 35.22(2)(a)) or file a § 57.081 insolvency certificate — nonpayment is non-jurisdictional (Rule 9.040(f)) but sanctionable by dismissal after 10 days' notice (Rule 9.410(a)). Indigency runs through the Pasco clerk (Rule 9.430(a); §§ 57.081/57.082, 200% FPG, ministerial determination, written reasons on denial, review by the lower tribunal then by motion in the 2DCA). Calendared from the June 24 notice: record index ~Aug 13, record transmission Mon. ## 30-day jurisdictional deadline
- AUTHORITY: Fla. R. App. P. 9.110(b), (d) [CONFIRMED against both the Jan. 1, 2024 flcourts.gov compilation and the July 1, 2026 Bar compilation]
- SAYS: "Jurisdiction of the court under this rule must be invoked by filing a notice with the clerk of the lower tribunal within 30 days of rendition of the order to be reviewed." The notice must substantially follow Form 9.900(a), attach a conformed copy of the order appealed, and be accompanied by the fee except as provided in Rule 9.430. The 30-day window is jurisdictional and cannot be extended.
- APPLIES: DIN 44 rendered June 18, 2026 (e-signed 5:00 p.m.); notice filed June 24 — timely. The original 30-day window ran to Monday July 20, 2026, so even the July 3 amended notice fell inside it.
- CONFIDENCE: high

## Rendition and tolling — a Rule 1.540 motion does NOT toll
- AUTHORITY: Fla. R. App. P. 9.020(h), (h)(1)(A)–(M), (h)(2); 9.130(a)(5) [CORRECTED — current 9.130(a)(5) (2025 amendment cycle) deleted the word 'therefore': "Motions for rehearing directed to these orders are not authorized under these rules and will not toll the time for filing a notice of appeal"]
- SAYS: "An order is rendered when a signed, written order is filed with the clerk of the lower tribunal." Only the listed motions toll rendition; a 1.540 motion is not among them. Orders on authorized, timely 1.540 motions are reviewable under Rule 9.130 (30 days; no rehearing tolling).
- APPLIES: The 1.540(b)(4) motion has no effect on the appeal's timeline. Any denial of it gets its own 30-day appeal window.
- CONFIDENCE: high

## Premature and amended notices — defects curable
- AUTHORITY: Fla. R. App. P. 9.110(l); 9.040(d); 9.040(f) [CONFIRMED]
- SAYS: A premature notice vests jurisdiction once a final order is rendered before dismissal (9.110(l)). "At any time in the interest of justice, the court may permit any part of the proceeding to be amended so that it may be disposed of on the merits" (9.040(d)). Fee/document failures are "not... jurisdictional" but sanctionable (9.040(f)).
- APPLIES: The July 3 amended notice (attaching DIN 44, correcting the reviewing court) + certified transmittal cured the original notice's defects — confirmed by the July 7 discharge order. Nothing cures an untimely original notice, but the original was timely.
- CONFIDENCE: high

## Why DCAs issue early show-cause orders, and what follows
- AUTHORITY: Fla. R. App. P. 9.410(a) (10 days' notice before court-initiated sanctions incl. dismissal); 9.110(l); 9.040(f); 9.040(b)(1) (transfer if wrong court) [rule text verified; 'typical triggers' = practice knowledge]
- SAYS: The OSC itself is the 10-day notice Rule 9.410(a) requires before court-initiated dismissal. Common triggers: apparent jurisdictional defect (nonfinal order, untimely notice, order not attached), unpaid fee without indigency determination, facially defective notice. After a response the court discharges, dismisses, transfers, or orders specific compliance.
- APPLIES: Exactly what happened: June 26 OSC (order appealed not attached) → July 3 response + amended notice → July 7 discharge. The June 26 FEE order remains open (40 days → ~Aug 5) — noncompliance with a fee order's date certain is the most common cause of pro se appeal dismissal. Comply or have the indigency certificate on file by the deadline.
- CONFIDENCE: medium (typicality); high (rule text)

## Proceeding as an indigent — statute and rule mechanics
- AUTHORITY: Fla. Stat. § 57.081(1), § 57.082 (2025); Fla. R. App. P. 9.430(a) [CONFIRMED verbatim]
- SAYS: § 57.081(1): an indigent party "shall receive the services of the courts, sheriffs, and clerks... despite his or her present inability to pay," covering filing fees and "any other cost or service arising out of pending litigation," upon certification under § 27.52 or § 57.082. § 57.082: apply to the CLERK; indigent if household income ≤ 200% of the federal poverty guidelines; "The determination of indigent status is a ministerial act of the clerk"; denial reviewable by the court on petition without a filing fee. Rule 9.430(a): the application goes to the clerk of the LOWER tribunal on the supreme-court-approved form; written reasons required for denial; review by the lower tribunal, then by motion in the appellate court.
- APPLIES: Her application (signed 7/7; determination block blank as of 7/10) is in the right place — confirm it is docketed, chase the determination, and if denied: written reasons → county-court review (free) → 9.430(a) motion in the 2DCA. Certification waives the $300 fee and clerk charges (it does NOT pay private transcriptionists — moot here since no recording exists). Watch § 57.082(2)'s asset presumption given the $18,000 home listing; rebut with no-title/no-access facts.
- CONFIDENCE: high

## Rule 9.300 — motions and extensions
- AUTHORITY: Fla. R. App. P. 9.300(a), (b), (d) [CONFIRMED]
- SAYS: Extension motions need a consultation certificate (pro se performs the consultation herself) and should propose a date certain; a timely-served motion TOLLS the schedule until disposition; an extension of the record automatically extends the next brief. Non-tolling motions include stay motions (d)(2) and sanctions motions (d)(9). Nothing extends the 30-day notice deadline.
- APPLIES: Expect to need an extension if the 9.200(b)(5) settlement runs long — confer with Wynne, file BEFORE Sept 2.
- CONFIDENCE: high

## Rule 9.410 sanctions — both directions
- AUTHORITY: Fla. R. App. P. 9.410(a), (b)(3)–(5) [CONFIRMED]
- SAYS: Court-initiated sanctions (after 10 days' notice) for frivolous/bad-faith filings include striking, dismissal, costs, fees. Party-initiated fee motions (e.g., § 57.105) must first be served on the target only, with a 21-DAY SAFE HARBOR to withdraw/correct before filing.
- APPLIES: Keep every 2DCA filing tethered to record facts and real authority. If served with a § 57.105/9.410(b) motion, use the 21-day safe harbor — never ignore it. Agency complaints (Bar, JQC, DBPR, AG) belong OUTSIDE the appeal; referencing them in briefs adds sanction risk without merit value.
- CONFIDENCE: high
