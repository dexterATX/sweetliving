## Core legal theories in play

0. **The Zielinski error** — in *Zielinski v. Palermo*, No. 2D2025-1009 (Fla. 2d DCA May 29, 2026), the 2DCA reversed **this same Pasco county court** for entering a default eviction judgment without an evidentiary hearing on a pending § 83.60(2) motion to determine rent. Rodgers filed her Emergency Motion to Determine Rent on June 1 — before the final judgment; DIN 41 ¶B denied it as "legally deficient" without a hearing. This is the strongest, most direct appellate issue.
1. **Wrong vehicle / void judgment** — a Chapter 83 residential eviction presupposes a landlord–tenant relationship; Rodgers claims there was none. If she is a mobile-home *purchaser* (Ch. 723 / title dispute), the default eviction judgment may be attackable as void (Rule 1.540(b)(4)) or reversible on appeal. Only a park owner can evict under § 723.061, ch. 82 is expressly inapplicable to park property (§ 82.02(2)), and § 319.22(1) bars recognizing untitled ownership claims — including Sweet Living's. The June 18 judgment (DIN 44) itself **found Rodgers "held valid title"** to the home.
2. **Default entered despite appearance** — she had filed answers (March 10/16), a verified answer, motions, and a jury demand before the defaults; Rule 1.500 and the *Stuart-Findlay*/Picchi line require notice and bar defaults over filed responsive papers.
3. **No record** — reconstruct via Rule 9.200(b)(5) statement; without it, Applegate-type affirmance risk.
4. **Stay** — denied June 30 and the writ executed July 9; the appeal is not moot (*Jappa*) and reversal supports vacatur of the writ (*Ford*).
5. **Judicial disqualification** — Rule 2.330 motion pending (ground: "Desmond needs his money" from the bench); confirm service on the judge; if denied, writ of prohibition.
6. **Money recovery** — title fraud ($18k paid; recorded $2,000 price; REBUILT/total-loss brands; false date): FLHSMV/State Attorney report, FDUTPA, civil theft/conversion (separate from the eviction case).
