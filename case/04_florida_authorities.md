# Florida Authorities — Verified Digest (researched & citation-checked July 10, 2026)

Every citation below was researched by dedicated agents and then **adversarially verified against official sources** — the 2025 Florida Statutes on Online Sunshine (leg.state.fl.us), the Florida Bar's official rules compilations current through July 1, 2026, and official court opinions. ## B. No transcript — reconstructing the record

| Authority | Verified point |
|---|---|
| Fla. R. No deadline tied to the brief in the Florida rule (the 14-day rule is federal FRAP 10(c) — don't import it). |
| Fla. R. App. P. 9.200(a)(1)–(3), (b)(1), (f) | Record automatically includes everything filed below; her July 3 designation/directions were timely (Jul 6 deadline after weekend roll); 9.200(f)(2) guarantees an opportunity to supplement before the appeal is decided on an incomplete record — but (f) cannot manufacture a never-made transcript. |

| Fla. R. Gen. Prac. & Jud. Admin. 2.535; 6th Cir. AO 2007-079 | County civil hearings are not reported at public expense; party recordings are never the official record — confirming the (b)(5) statement is the only route. ## F. Judicial disqualification (Rule 2.330 — current lettering!)

| Authority | Verified point |
|---|---|
| Rule 2.330(c), (d), (e), (g) | Motion must be **sworn by the party**, state the **precise date of discovery**, be filed within **20 days** of discovery, and be **served on the judge** (Rule 2.516) — service starts the clock. Grounds are at **(e)** now. DIN 59 satisfies the content rules; its service-on-judge line is blank — cure immediately. |
| Rule 2.330(h); Bundy v. Rudd, 366 So. 2d 440 (Fla. 1978) | Judge rules on **legal sufficiency only**, facts taken as true; an order that argues with the allegations is itself a **new ground**. |

## H. Guardrails (read before filing anything)

- **§ 57.105 & Rule 9.410**: fee-shifting/sanctions apply to pro se parties personally; 21-day safe harbor if a motion is served on her. Highest-risk genre: unsworn "notice and demand"/ownership-declaration filings — **file none of these in either court**. The 1.540(b)(4) motion and 2.330 motion are recognized, low-risk vehicles when kept fact-based.
- **Consistency**: AG/FDACS/DBPR complaints are public records Wynne can pull — every complaint must match the sworn court filings word-for-word.
- **Bar/JQC**: Bar complaints are absolutely privileged if kept inside the grievance process (Tobkin v. Jarboe, 710 So. 2d 975 (Fla. 1998)) but the Bar defers during litigation and needs proof of *knowing* falsity; the JQC handles ethics, not legal error, and cannot touch the case. Lowest priority.

Pro Se Appellate Handbook (flabarappellate.org, 2023). Florida Free Legal Answers. Bar LRS $25 consult (800) 342-8011.
